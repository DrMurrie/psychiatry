Cardiology for Psychiatrists A practical refresher and reference

How to use this guide

  • Read in order: each section builds on the one before. By the end you should be able to read most ECGs systematically and understand most common cardiac conditions.
  • Sections 1-2 are physiology and ECG fundamentals. If you already feel solid, skim them and move on, but the framing reappears later.
  • Sections 3-7 are the major disease areas. Each topic is self-contained for later reference.
  • Section 8 is the psychiatry-cardiology interface (QT, antipsychotics, clozapine, lithium, ECT). Read it after the rest, it will make much more sense.
Disclaimer. Educational. Not a substitute for cardiology advice on a specific patient. Australian context (PBS, RACGP, Therapeutic Guidelines, NHF) referenced where relevant.
